Introducing Myself

04 Apr 2014

My name is Ruben Caliandro (a.k.a. Chosko), and I am a Computer Science student at the University of Turin (Italy).

I like computers since I was a child, because my father used to spend some of his free time playing videogames and reading IT journals. I just watched and learned everything he did.

My passion for computer graphics started in 2005 (15 years old), when Stefano Nada sent me a picture of the Utah teapot with a nice mix of lights, reflections and HDR. Since that time, we started studying computer graphics, learning C#, DirectX 9.0c and HLSL.

Until I went to University (in 2009), we worked on some personal projects. The most important was a simple framework based on DirectX, which I also used for my thesis ("Mathematics applied to the 3D Computer Graphics Programming") at the end of High School. During the final exam, as a demo I presented a simulation of the solar system, entirely made with our framework.

This is a screenshot of the simulation presented for my thesis at the High School

Later I started University (Computer Science), and meanwhile I also worked 3 years in a company as Web Developer. In these years I put aside Computer Graphics and focused on Java, C, PHP, SQL (MySQL) and the main client-side languages ([X]HTML, CSS, Javascript).

After I graduated (B.Sc.) in 2012, I enrolled in the Master of Science program (Virtual Reality) and at the same time I started working for another company, still as Web Developer. This time I met Ruby on Rails, that totally changed my way of working on Web.

Lately I resumed my passion for Computer Graphics, especially for Game Development, and I have been involved in projects with XNA (MonoGame) and Unity3D.
